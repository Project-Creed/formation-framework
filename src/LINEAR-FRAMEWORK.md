# Formation Linear Framework

The Formation Framework moves from how people are shaped to how mature people transmit responsibility.

## Phase One: See How Formation Works

The reader begins by recognizing that people are formed through example, habit, attention, environment, correction, affection, and security before they are formed by abstract agreement.

Chapters: 01-06.

## Phase Two: Respect Life Stages

The reader then considers childhood, adolescence, adulthood, elderhood, parenting, and education. Formation changes across stages, but every stage carries responsibility appropriate to capacity and role.

Chapters: 07-12.

## Phase Three: Build Character In Culture

The reader applies formation to conscience, courage, restraint, service, peer norms, mentorship, culture, ritual, and rites of passage. Character is formed socially as well as personally.

Chapters: 13-18.

## Phase Four: Repair And Transmit

The final movement concerns technology, failure, institutional formation, intergenerational transmission, reforming malformation, and maturity. The test is whether people and institutions can correct what deforms and pass on conditions that form responsibility.

Chapters: 19-24.

## Operating Cadence

Use this cadence when applying the framework:

1. Name the formation pattern.
2. Identify what behavior it actually produces.
3. Identify what is being modeled.
4. Identify what is being practiced repeatedly.
5. Reverse roles with the person being formed or corrected.
6. Name what needs repair or reinforcement.
7. Choose one formative practice.
8. Review what the pattern becomes over time.
