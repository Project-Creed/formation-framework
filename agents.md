# Repository Goal

Create a practical framework for character, education, and generational formation downstream of Ethosism. The Formation Framework applies objective reality, reciprocity, integrity, long-term responsibility, and contribution to childhood, adolescence, adulthood, elderhood, parenting, education, habit, correction, mentorship, culture, technology, institutions, repair, and intergenerational transmission.

# Writing Direction

When writing or revising this book, follow [docs/writing-guide.md](docs/writing-guide.md). Keep the Formation Framework grounded in observable reality, human development, reciprocal moral reasoning, household and institutional responsibility, correction, repair, and contribution across generations.

The work should be secular and non-theological: it may draw respectfully from religious, philosophical, scientific, educational, civic, and practical traditions, but it must not depend on revelation, clergy, supernatural claims, partisan identity, or any single theology for its authority.

Prefer concrete, defensible claims over vague inspiration. Claims about formation should be testable against behavior, developmental reality, role reversal, incentives, repair, and decade-scale responsibility.

The repo-local Codex skill for this work lives at [.agents/skills/formation-writing](.agents/skills/formation-writing). Keep Formation-specific Codex skills in this repository rather than installing them globally.

# Review Workflow

When reviewing or revising a chapter with the user, proceed paragraph by paragraph. Present one paragraph at a time, wait for the user's edits or approval, apply edits to that paragraph, then move to the next paragraph in the chapter.

Do not batch-review an entire chapter unless the user explicitly asks for a full-pass review. The default review method for this book is interactive paragraph-level editing.
